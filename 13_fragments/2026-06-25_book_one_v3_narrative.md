  # UMADA Book One Canon Timeline

  ## Two Years Before Cindy

  A peaceful alien federation arrives on Earth for disclosure. It is not an invasion and not an infestation. Some members remain on Earth and settle in remote places for peaceful living.

  ## Cindy

  Cindy alters the atmosphere and leaves sediment across the planet. The environmental consequences last for centuries.

  ## The Cape Operation

  Ada and KC are assigned to the Cape operation. They are meant to meet at the Friedmandestorp bread stand before proceeding together. Ada arrives early at 8:00 a.m. because she wants fresh bread. KC is expected later.

  That same morning, Tallai is permitted to leave the chimera refuge to get bread.

  ## Cape Wipeout

  The strike occurs before the intended sequence. Ada is caught at the epicenter. KC is separated. Tallai discovers Ada alive.

  ## Dead Glass

  The built environment fails. Glass, concrete, electronics, infrastructure, and communications collapse into a planetary aftermath. Dead Glass becomes a ubiquitous programmable substrate.

  ## The Great Scientific Retreat

  After the blast, public trust in chimeric and scientific labs collapses. Funding becomes extinct. Scientists scatter and knowledge moves into hidden communities, oral traditions, memory tablets, and sanctuary networks.

  ## The Sanctuary Network

  Chimeras are hunted. Ada's estate becomes a refuge network. Tallai becomes Steward of the Estate. Nago becomes a secret survival language.

  ## Rise of Nago

  Nago evolves from refuge communication into the universal civic language. It is visual, tactile, signed, spoken, sung, carved, and remembered.

  ## Rise of UMADA

  Region One becomes UMADA, "It will be well." Its capital is Tisetan, "It is done."

  ## Two Centuries Later

  The Five Houses convene. Ada refuses to become the time traveler. She chooses a Reporter and sends them with the Omoluabi device to witness history, not change it.

  # Nago Script — Semantic Multimodal Language

  Nago is not an alphabet. It is a semantic writing and communication system.

  Each glyph represents a core concept rather than a sound. Meaning emerges through relationship, sequence, gesture, position, touch, song, and context.

  ## Equal Modalities

  Nago exists as:

  - visual glyphs
  - tactile glyphs
  - Hand Relay / sign language
  - spoken language
  - sung forms
  - carved tablets
  - memory stones
  - Dead Glass inscriptions

  No single modality is the real Nago. They are all Nago.

  ## Canonical Root Glyphs v0.1

  | Glyph Concept | Meaning |
  |---|---|
  | Circle over crescent | Community / Home |
  | Three horizontal bars / staff form | Governance / Law |
  | Concentric target | Memory |
  | Eye | Observation |
  | Spiral diamond | Knowledge / Inquiry |
  | Dotted matrix | People / Community |
  | Branch | Growth / Life |
  | Vertical staff | Stewardship |
  | Divided circle | Balance |

  ## Combination Examples

  | Glyph Combination | Interpretation |
  |---|---|
  | Observation + Memory | Record |
  | Community + Memory | History |
  | Community + Stewardship | Governance |
  | Growth + Memory | Learn |
  | Stewardship + Balance | Justice |

  ## Design Principle

  A language that cannot be accessed by all citizens cannot govern all citizens.

  # Nago Memory Tablets

  Memory Tablets preserve Nago as a living archive.

  They are not read only left-to-right. They are read holistically.

  ## Registers

  - Central register: principal concepts.
  - Surrounding text: context, commentary, history, song, or law.
  - Bottom row: glyph reference, teaching row, or dictionary line.

  ## Educational Use

  Children first learn core glyphs. Later they learn combinations, oral traditions, tactile readings, sign equivalents, and historical commentaries.

  ## Canonical Interpretation

  Memory Tablets are not only storage. They are civic pedagogy. They teach a population how to remember together.
