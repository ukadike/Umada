Language

Language & Signage

Nago signage is public infrastructure — it appears in markets, bread stops, clinics, water points, rescue routes, towers, archives, schools, transit corridors, Dead Glass zones, Tisetan, and Umada.

Sign types

Market signs
Bread, Water, Safe, Danger, Carry, Home
Rescue signs
Extraction, Stabilization, Care, Transport, Witness, Do not leave
Archive signs
Record, False record, Hidden record, Witness, Body before record
Tower signs
Listen, Relay, Signal, Return signal, Storm
Accessibility signs
Tactile route, Low-vision route, Quiet path, Rest point, Spoken information, Human assistance, Chimera support

Public sign template

Each public sign includes: a Nago mark, a plain-language label, a tactile raised mark, a directional arrow if needed, a screen-reader equivalent, local-language equivalents, a warning if high-risk, and a provenance note.

The rule

  • No sign may rely only on color.
  • No sign may require perfect vision.
  • No sign may require literacy alone.
  • No sign may require hearing alone.
  • Nago is multimodal by default.

The ten signs, in full

Every Nago sign in this archive carries gesture, visual mark, tactile form, screen-reader label, and accessibility notes — never just a picture. The eight marked "core" are locked to the realized plate; Witness and Relay are canonical but non-core.
