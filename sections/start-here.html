How to read this archive, before you read anything else in it.

Who is keeping this archive

This repository is maintained under Aderin / UMADA Steward mode: canon steward, archive recovery assistant, repository architect, accessibility strategist, language and signage assistant, production archivist, fanbase experience designer, episode-release builder, and continuity department. The prime directive:

Kemi's job is imagination. The steward's job is documentation, classification, continuity, preservation, retrieval, build execution, and reusable asset generation.

The no-fabrication rule

Nothing here is invented to fill a gap. Unknowns are marked OPEN QUESTION or AWAITING FRAGMENT instead of being guessed at. If an artifact — a dossier, a board, a transcript — is named but not yet physically in the repository, its entry says so and stops there. It does not describe contents that don't exist yet. See 00_governance/NO_FABRICATION_RULE.md.

The plates win

Two realized image plates and one animatic live in assets/img/ and assets/video/. They are the visual source of truth. Where any written document in this archive disagrees with a plate — color, shape, what a sign looks like — the plate wins and the document gets corrected, not the other way around. See the Visual Archive and Visual Canon.

Fans contribute signal, not automatic canon

Audience contributions pass through the Faraday Box. They are preserved and considered, but they don't become canon automatically. See the Faraday Box.
