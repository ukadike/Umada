Nago Symbology

The visual operating system of Umada. Nago begins before grammar — need comes first.

"Nago" draws on Nagô / Yoruba heritage. Within UMADA it is a fictional, evolving survival-and-care sign system — not a depiction of any real sign language or sacred script. Every rooted term is treated with provenance and care.

Nago was born from the hands of survivors. It was shaped in markets, shelters, and ruins. It was carried by Tallai, Ada, Q, and the people. It belongs to everyone.

Design principles

  • Accessible to all
  • Easy to learn
  • Easy to feel
  • Easy to see
  • Easy to remember
  • Easy to share

Two axes — never merged

Function families organize signage (what a sign is for). Motif families organize narrative visual identity (title cards, dossier boards, brand iconography). They are not the same system and are never collapsed into one.

      Visual grammar

      Built on the Golden Rectangle and ancient geometry — for legibility and memory, not mystification.

        Tactile system

        Every Nago sign must exist as: gesture, flat visual mark, raised tactile mark, verbal description, screen-reader label, public signage icon, map symbol, episode graphic.

        • Avoid thin details; use clear edges and high-contrast relief.
        • Support fingertip scanning.
        • Use redundant text labels.
        • Never rely on color alone.
        • Design for low vision and blind users from the beginning.
