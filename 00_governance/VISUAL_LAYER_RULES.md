# Visual Layer Rules

UMADA has two distinct visual layers. Never blend them.

## 1. Presentation / archive layer

The chrome of the site itself: navigation, headers, governance pages, canon pages,
the archive frame around everything. This layer reads as a monochrome graphite archive.

- Paper: `#efece2`
- Ink: `#1a1a1a`
- Line: `#d0cdc6`
- Muted: `#6f6f6f`
- Type: Helvetica Neue (UI/sans), Georgia (long-form/serif reading), Courier Prime
  (annotations, metadata, status tags, code-like fragments).

This layer never goes full-color. It is the frame the world is viewed through — a
recovered, continuity-department, archive aesthetic.

## 2. Diegetic / world layer

Content that depicts the world itself — illustrations, episode stills, the Nago plates,
the animatic, photography of in-world signage — is colorful where canon supports it.

- The world is southern-African coastal: Southern Cape corridor, near Cape Agulhas.
- Colorful, weather-rich, painted. Harbor light, storm light, market color.
- **Never Arctic.** No ice, snow, glacier, or Nordic/Greenland framing, ever — this was a
  reintroduced error in a prior revision (see `00_governance/CANON_STATUS.md`) and is
  permanently retired.
- Real-world place or palette references used as internal mood-board shorthand stay
  internal notes. They are never named in canon text or public-facing copy.

## Where the layers meet

Diegetic images (the plates, the animatic, episode stills) are framed by the
presentation layer's monochrome chrome — caption blocks, alt text, metadata — but the
image content itself stays in the world's own color. Do not desaturate diegetic art to
match the chrome, and do not let UI chrome borrow world color.

## Source of truth

Where a written document and a present plate in `assets/img/` disagree about anything
visual — color, shape, composition, what a sign looks like — the plate wins.
