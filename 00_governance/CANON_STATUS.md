# Canon Status Ledger

This file is the single place where locked spellings and decisions are recorded so they
cannot drift or be reintroduced as variants. If a doc elsewhere disagrees with this
ledger, this ledger wins — except where the realized plates in `assets/img/` are the
named visual source of truth, in which case the plates win and this ledger is updated
to match them.

Status legend: **LOCKED** / **PROBABLE** / **EMERGING** / **OPEN QUESTION** / **RETIRED**

## Locked spellings

- **Friedmandostorp** — canonical settlement spelling (with an "o" in the middle syllable:
  "...mand**o**storp"). The variant **"Friedmandestorp"** (an "e" in that syllable) is
  RETIRED and must not be reintroduced — it has now resurfaced three times: a 2026-06-22
  repository-restructuring brief, the 2026-06-25 "UMADA Book One" canon package, and the
  2026-06-25 `UMADA_CANON_INDEX.md` directive itself. Each time it has been corrected back
  to the locked spelling. Kemi confirmed directly on 2026-06-25 that "Friedmandestorp" is
  a typo in all three sources, including her own Canon Index, and is not a canon change.
- **Jackal Peoples** — canonical faction-layer name. The variant **"Jackal Tribes"** is
  RETIRED for the same reason (2026-06-22 brief). See Open Question #34 for its
  relationship, if any, to the new **Jaguar House** (below) — the two are NOT treated as
  the same name; one is not silently merged into the other.
- **Tallai / Talla** — both forms attested in source canon; treated as the same person
  pending confirmation of a single preferred form. See Open Questions.
- **Sisekong / Tishekong** — RETIRED as transcription errors. The correct names are
  **Umada** and **Tisetan**.

## Locked geography

- The world is **southern-African coastal** — Southern Cape corridor, near Cape Agulhas.
- Friedmandostorp is a colorful, weather-rich harbor settlement with Nigerian-diaspora
  Agege-bread influence.
- **Never Arctic.** Any "Ilulissat-inspired" or Greenland framing is RETIRED — it was a
  reintroduced error in a prior revision and has been removed. This is a permanent
  guardrail, not a style preference.
- Real-world place/palette references used as internal mood-board shorthand (e.g. for
  color or texture comparison) stay internal; they are never named in canon text or
  public-facing materials.

## Locked frame narrative

- UMADA opens through a future reporter / time traveler carrying an **Omoluabi device**.
- Omoluabi is not the story; it is the device that lets the story be witnessed.
- The future council dispute concerns leadership of **Umada and Tisetan**.
- Opening beat: tick, tick, tick, tick — BOOM.
- The Reporter / Time Traveler is a distinct character from Ada — chosen by Ada for the
  task, not Ada herself. Ada refuses to become the time traveler. (Source: `Book One`
  canon package and `UMADA_CANON_INDEX.md`, both 2026-06-25; corroborated by both

## Locked Nago structure

- Nago has **two axes** that must never be merged:
  - **Function families (signage, seven):** Survival, Care, Signal, Movement, Civic,
    **Memory**, **Environment**.
  - **Motif families (narrative, five):** Wound, Relay, Archive, Storm, Visitor.
- A **third, separate layer** — semantic root glyphs (concept-glyphs like Memory,
  Observation, Stewardship, Governance/Law) — was added 2026-06-25 from the Book One
  canon package's `nago_script.md`. It is EMERGING/draft, sourced from a single fragment,
  not yet checked against the realized plates, and is kept in its own file
  (`07_languages/NAGO_ROOT_GLYPHS_DRAFT.md` / `data/nago_root_glyphs.json`) rather than
  merged into either of the two locked axes above.
- "Witness" → "Memory" is recorded as a **rename**, not a deletion. Witness's signs are
  folded under the Memory family. "Environment" is **new** at EMERGING status with no
  signs seeded yet — none invented to fill it.
- **Core survival signs (locked to the realized plate, eight):** Bread, Water, Safe,
  Danger, Carry, Home, **Help**, **Rest**.
- Witness and Relay are canonical Nago signs but are **not** part of the core-survival
  set; they belong to the Memory and Signal families respectively.
- The realized plates — `assets/img/PLATE_NAGO_SYMBOLOGY_OF_UMADA.png` and
  `assets/img/PLATE_PROTO_NAGO.png` — are the visual source of truth for Nago. Where any
  document disagrees with a plate, the plate wins and the document is corrected.
- "Nago" carries a real-world provenance note: it draws on Nagô / Yoruba heritage. Within
  UMADA it is a fictional, evolving survival-and-care sign system, not a depiction of any
  real sign language or sacred script.
- Nago's modalities are locked as: spoken, signed, sung, tactile, visual glyphs, carved,
  programmable (Dead Glass inscriptions, memory stones). No modality is secondary —
  accessibility defines the language. (`UMADA_CANON_INDEX.md`, corroborated by the Book
  One package's `nago_script.md`: "No single modality is the real Nago. They are all
  Nago.")
- **Hand Relay** is locked as the signed/gesture modality of Nago, and must explicitly
  account for people with limited hand mobility through prosthetics, alternate gestures,
  tactile, or cybernetic supports.

## New events, added 2026-06-25 (per `UMADA_CANON_INDEX.md` and the Book One canon package)

- **Quiet Arrival** — two years before Cindy, a peaceful interstellar federation arrives
  on Earth for disclosure; not an invasion. Some visitors remain in remote areas. Both
  new sources agree on this.
- **Cindy** — both new sources independently describe Cindy as an atmospheric event that
  spreads sediment/altered material-biological possibilities, occurring *before* the
  Cape Operation and Cape Wipeout, with effects "lasting for centuries." See **Open
  Question #32** — this directly conflicts with the already-locked civilizational arc
  below, which places Cindy *after* Cape Wipeout, carrying a breach that follows from it.
  Neither account is altered here; the conflict is flagged, not resolved.
- **Great Scientific Retreat** — after Cape Wipeout, public trust in chimeric/scientific
  labs collapses, funding disappears, and knowledge survives in distributed communities,
  hidden archives, oral tradition, and sanctuary networks. Consistent with and extends
  the existing EMERGING Era framing of the same period.

## Locked political/species structure — Five Houses (added 2026-06-25)

- `UMADA_CANON_INDEX.md` locks **exactly five Houses** "unless explicitly revised": Lion
  House, Jaguar House, Cybernetic House, Pure Human House, S-Vector House — see
  `data/five_houses.json` for the full structured form.
- This is corroborated independently by the Book One canon package's `book/timeline.md`
  ("The Five Houses convene" at the Council Era), giving two independent sources from
  Kemi for the same five-House structure.
- This is layered **on top of**, not in place of, the existing EMERGING six-name faction
  list for Era 15 / The Kingdom Age (Lion Kingdom, Jackal Peoples, Black Stars, Phoenix
  Rising, Cybernetic Houses, Pure Human Communities) in `data/factions.json` and
  `01_canon/CIVILIZATIONAL_LEDGER.md`. Three names plausibly correspond (Lion
  Kingdom/Lion House, Cybernetic Houses/Cybernetic House, Pure Human
  Communities/Pure Human House); two Houses (Jaguar, S-Vector) have no clear predecessor
  in the old list; two old names (Black Stars, Phoenix Rising) have no clear Houses
  counterpart. None of these correspondences are asserted as fact — see Open Questions
  #33 and #34.
- **Lion** and **Jaguar** are locked as the first and second successful chimera
  lineages respectively, stabilized in the "immediate aftermath" of Cape Wipeout using
  Cindy's sediment. Whether Tallai, Q, or Quartz belong to either named lineage is not
  stated anywhere and is not assumed — see Open Question #31.
- **S-Vector House** ("alien federation descendants, ancient knowledge, memory stones,
  disclosure") substantially clarifies the previously-undefined `Era 2 — S-Vector`
  (PROBABLE) and connects it to the new Quiet Arrival event. The link is inferred, not
  stated outright in either source — see Open Question #35.
