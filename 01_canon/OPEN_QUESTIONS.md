# Open Questions

A living register. Nothing below is invented — each item is either explicitly flagged as
open in a source document, or is a real gap noticed while reconciling the source package
against the realized plates. See `00_governance/TINY_QUESTIONS_PROTOCOL.md` for how the
smallest of these get asked to Kemi one at a time.

## Naming & canon-structure decisions (Kemi's to confirm or change)

1. **Memory vs Witness.** Applied as a rename ("Witness" → "Memory" family) per the
   refined plate. If Witness and Memory were meant as two distinct families, say so and
   they'll be split.
2. **Environment family contents.** Seeded as EMERGING with no signs yet — no canon
   existed to seed from.
3. **Motif-five fate.** The five motif families (Wound, Relay, Archive, Storm, Visitor)
   are kept as a live narrative axis, separate from the seven signage families. Could be
   retired in favor of the plate's Visual Grammar primitives (Circle, Open Circle,
   Broken Circle, Three Rings, Hand+Ring) if Kemi prefers.
4. **Talla vs Tallai.** Both forms are attested. Is this one character with a
   nickname/full-name pair, or a spelling that needs to converge on one form?

## Nago / signage

5. **Proto-Nago marks that didn't carry forward.** `PLATE_PROTO_NAGO` documents fourteen
   marks (including Go This Way, Care/Love, Look/Watch, Gather, Down/Below, Up/Above,
   Stop/No); the refined plate's core set is eight (seven of the fourteen carried
   forward, plus Rest, which has no proto-layer precursor). Were the seven that didn't
   carry forward dropped, absorbed into the seven function families some other way, or
   simply not yet re-rendered in the refined system?
6. **Visual Grammar primitives as buildable shapes.** The plate shows Circle, Open
   Circle, Broken Circle, Three Rings, and Hand+Ring constructed against the Golden
   Rectangle, but no vector/CSS-level shape spec exists yet for the site to render them
   precisely rather than approximate them.
7. **Quartz absent from the plate's Honor & Origin line.** The refined plate credits
   Nago as "carried by Tallai, Ada, Q, and the people," but Quartz is locked elsewhere
   as a fourth Cape Wipeout survivor and load-bearer/rescue anchor. Intentional omission,
   or an artifact of the plate predating Quartz's full lock-in?
8. **What mark would Tallai recognize first?** (Symbology Bible tiny question, not
   resolved by either plate.)

## Frame narrative & geography

9. **The council dispute over Umada and Tisetan.** Locked as a fact of the frame
   narrative, but who the disputants are and what's actually contested is not specified
   anywhere in the source package.
10. **Relationship between Tisetan and Umada.** Are they sister cities, a city and its
    successor, rival capitals, or something else? Both are named as future
    signal/civic centers but their relationship to each other isn't defined.
11. **What the Omoluabi device looks like or how it works.** Locked as the device that
    lets the story be witnessed; no physical or functional description exists.
12. **What ADA-1 is.** Ethical principles are attributed to "ADA-1" (Trust Body Before
    Record, etc.), but whether ADA-1 is an AI system, a doctrine, a person, or a
    designation is never stated.

## Timeline & world mechanics

13. **Mechanism of the Breach.** How containment failure turns "the Atlantic into a
    carrier" is named but not explained.
14. **Mechanism of Super Hurricane Cindy as a carrier event.** Cindy carries "the breach
    through water, air, and storm" — the physical/biological mechanism isn't specified.
15. **Chimeran Flu origin and transmission.** Named as the Cape Wipeout outbreak; origin
    and transmission mechanics are open.
16. **Angel Dust — relationship and naming.** Named as an evolution of Chimeran Flu
    during a period of instability. Flagging for provenance/care given the real-world
    drug-slang homonym, the same way Nago carries a real-world provenance note — worth
    Kemi's confirmation on whether the resonance is intentional.
17. **Dead Glass — nature of the collapse.** Named as network/infrastructure/information
    collapse; whether "glass" is literal, technological, or metaphorical isn't specified.
18. **S-Vector.** Connects biology, technology, memory, adaptation, and consciousness per
    `FOUNDATIONAL_STRATA.md`; no further definition exists.
19. **Hidden Archive / alien presence.** PROBABLE status only; no specifics beyond
    "fragmented evidence, suppressed record, misunderstood myth."
20. **Atlantis's relationship to the main timeline.** Era 0 names it as remembered
    catastrophe; how (or whether) it surfaces in Cape Wipeout–era or later storytelling
    is undefined.
